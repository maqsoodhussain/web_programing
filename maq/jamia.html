Jamia Masjid, Srinagar

Jamia Masjid in Srinagar is one of the most prominent and historic mosques in Jammu and Kashmir. Located in the heart of the old city, this grand mosque is an epitome of Indo-Saracenic architecture.

Built in 1394 AD by Sultan Sikandar, and later expanded by his son, Zain-ul-Abidin, the mosque is known for its 378 wooden pillars, each made from a single piece of deodar wood, and its spacious courtyard.

The serene ambiance of Jamia Masjid offers a peaceful retreat, making it a spiritual haven for thousands of worshippers and visitors alike.

Despite being over 600 years old, the mosque has withstood the test of time, surviving numerous calamities and remaining a symbol of resilience and faith in the region.

Visitors to Srinagar are drawn to this magnificent mosque not only for its spiritual significance but also for its stunning architecture and historical importance.
